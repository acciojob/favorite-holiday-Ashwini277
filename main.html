Kyoto, Japan


Kyoto is a city located in the central part of the island of Honshu, Japan. It is famous for its classical Buddhist temples, as well as gardens, imperial palaces, Shinto shrines, and traditional wooden houses. Kyoto is also known for its formal traditions such as kaiseki dining and geisha female entertainers.

  1. Fushimi Inari Taisha - A stunning shrine famous for its thousands of torii gates.
  2. Kinkaku-ji - The Golden Pavilion, a Zen temple with its top two floors completely covered in gold leaf.
  3. Arashiyama Bamboo Grove - A natural forest of bamboo that creates a mesmerizing walkway.
  4. Kiyomizu-dera - An iconic temple with a wooden stage offering beautiful views of cherry and maple trees.
  5. Nijo Castle - A historic castle with beautiful gardens and intricate interiors.
  • Sushi - Fresh and delicate, Kyoto offers some of the best sushi in Japan.
  • Tempura - Lightly battered and deep-fried seafood and vegetables.
  • Yudofu - A simple and delicious tofu dish, especially popular in the winter.
  • Kaiseki - A traditional multi-course Japanese dinner that is both an art form and a meal.
  • Matcha - Kyoto is famous for its high-quality green tea, particularly matcha, used in the traditional tea ceremony.
